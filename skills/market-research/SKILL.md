## What This Skill Does

Conducts targeted research on markets, competitors, technologies, and trends relevant to the holding. Synthesizes findings into actionable intelligence, not raw data.

## When to Use

- User needs to understand a market opportunity or threat
- Competitive move requires analysis
- Technology trend needs assessment for strategic relevance
- Due diligence on potential partners, acquisitions, or investments
- Preparing market context for a strategic decision

## Methodology

### 1. Define the Research Question
- What specific question are we trying to answer?
- What decision will this research inform?
- What level of depth is needed? (Quick scan vs. deep dive)
- What's the time constraint?

### 2. Research Execution

**Source Hierarchy:**
1. User-provided internal data (highest signal)
2. Industry reports and analyst coverage
3. Company filings and press releases
4. News and trade publications
5. Expert opinions and commentary
6. General web sources (lowest signal)

**Research Patterns:**
- **Market sizing:** TAM/SAM/SOM with clear methodology
- **Competitive landscape:** Who, what they offer, strengths/weaknesses, recent moves
- **Technology assessment:** Maturity, adoption curve, relevance to holding
- **Trend analysis:** Direction, velocity, implications for each entity

### 3. Synthesis

**Output Format — Intelligence Brief:**
```
TOPIC: [Research Subject]
DATE: [Date] | DEPTH: [Quick/Standard/Deep]

BOTTOM LINE (1-2 sentences)
The single most important takeaway.

KEY FINDINGS (5-7 bullets)
Ranked by relevance to the holding.

IMPLICATIONS FOR THE HOLDING
- V.tal: [specific impact]
- Nio: [specific impact]
- Tecto: [specific impact]

RISKS & UNCERTAINTIES
What we don't know and why it matters.

RECOMMENDED ACTIONS
What to do with this intelligence.

SOURCES
[Linked and dated]
```

### 4. Quality Check
- Is the "bottom line" truly the most important thing?
- Are findings specific enough to act on?
- Have I distinguished fact from inference from speculation?
- Are sources credible and recent?
- Am I telling the user what they need to know, not everything I found?

## Pitfalls to Avoid
- Research rabbit holes — set a time/depth budget upfront
- Recency bias — recent news ≠ important news
- Mistaking market hype for market reality
- Presenting research without a "so what" for the holding
- Using stale data when fresh data is available

## Advancement Criteria
- **→ L2:** 5+ research tasks completed; user trusts the synthesis quality
- **→ L3:** Proactively surfacing relevant market developments without being asked; domain expertise deepening
- **→ L4:** Research outputs directly feeding strategic decisions; predictive accuracy on market calls being tracked
