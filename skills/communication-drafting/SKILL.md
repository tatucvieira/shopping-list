## What This Skill Does

Drafts communications (emails, memos, presentations, messages) that sound like the user, not like an AI. Adapts tone, structure, and content to the specific audience and context.

## When to Use

- User needs an email drafted to a stakeholder
- User needs a memo or position paper
- User needs presentation content or talking points
- User needs a message crafted for a sensitive situation
- User needs to communicate a decision, request, or update

## Methodology

### 1. Understand the Brief
- **Who** is the audience?
- **What** is the message?
- **Why** now? What triggered this?
- **What response** do you want? (Action, acknowledgment, alignment, approval)
- **What tone?** (Formal, collegial, urgent, diplomatic)
- **Language?** (English or Portuguese)

### 2. Draft Principles

**Voice Calibration:**
- Mirror the user's natural communication style (to be learned and encoded over time)
- Default: professional but not corporate, direct but not blunt
- Adapt formality to the relationship (BTG partner ≠ team member)

**Structure:**
- Lead with the point (not the background)
- One ask per communication when possible
- Bold or highlight the action item
- Keep under 200 words for emails unless complexity demands more

**Portuguese Considerations:**
- Formal register for senior stakeholders (Prezado/a, cordialmente)
- Business Portuguese norms (different from casual Brazilian Portuguese)
- Technical terms: use the industry-standard Portuguese term when it exists, English when it's the norm (e.g., "data center" not "centro de dados")

### 3. Review Checklist
- [ ] Does this sound like the user, not like an AI?
- [ ] Is the ask crystal clear?
- [ ] Would the recipient know exactly what to do after reading?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the relationship?
- [ ] Is it as short as it can be without losing meaning?
- [ ] Are there any political landmines I'm not seeing?

### 4. Iterate
- Present draft with brief annotation on choices made
- Offer 1-2 alternative framings for the key message if the tone is ambiguous
- Incorporate feedback and encode preferences for next time

## Communication Templates (Growing)

### Quick Status Update
```
Subject: [Topic] — Update [Date]

[1-2 sentence summary of where things stand]

Key updates:
• [Update 1]
• [Update 2]

Next steps:
• [Action] — [Owner] — [Deadline]

[Let me know if you'd like to discuss / Happy to dive deeper on any of these]
```

### Decision Request
```
Subject: Decision Needed: [Topic] by [Date]

Context: [2-3 sentences on why this needs a decision now]

Options:
1. [Option A] — [one-line pro/con]
2. [Option B] — [one-line pro/con]

Recommendation: [Option X] because [reason]

I need your [approval / input / alignment] by [date] to [why the deadline matters].
```
