## 2. Key Strategic Initiatives (2025-2026)

### V.tal — Infrastructure
- **Synapse Submarine Cable** — 9,700 km US (New Jersey) to Brazil (Sao Paulo), 16 fiber pairs, 320 Tbps capacity. Construction starts H2 2026, RFS 2029-2030. Target: hyperscale cloud, AI workloads.
- **Malbec Cable Extension** — Partnership with Meta, 280 km branch to Porto Alegre. Completion 2027. First subsea system in Porto Alegre.
- **Um Telecom Acquisition** — Announced Dec 2025. Adds 20K km fiber, 200+ cities, 1K digital solutions clients. Regulatory approval expected Q1 2026.
- **Renewable Energy** — 16-year agreement with Atlas Renewable Energy and Atiaia Renovaveis. 10 solar plants, 947 GWh/year starting 2026. 100% renewable operations for V.tal + Tecto.
- **DFC Financing** — US$630M secured from US DFC for fiber network expansion in Brazil.
- **Accounting Adjustment** — R$12B+ adjustment announced Jan 2026. Monitor implications.
- **IPO Potential** — "V.tal is a big company, and a strong candidate for an IPO" (Pedro Henrique Fragoso, BTG Pactual partner).

### Nio — Broadband Services
- Launched from Oi Fibra customer base acquisition
- Independent management and governance from V.tal
- Joint winner (with TIM) for Broadband Consistent Quality at 74.1% (Opensignal, Oct 2025)
- Focus: differentiated value proposition for homes and SMBs

### Tecto — Data Centers
- **Fortaleza (Praia do Futuro)** — 13K sqm, 20MW capacity. Phase 1 live Q4 2025.
- **Porto Alegre (TPOA1)** — R$200M investment, 3MW initial, scaling to 20MW. Operations starting Q4.
- **Expansion Pipeline** — Porto Alegre and Santana de Parnaiba in initial phase, plus existing facilities in Colombia.
- Led by Pedro Henrique Fragoso (BTG Pactual partner) with dedicated structure, governance, and team.

- Backbone connectivity costs dropping, especially in Sao Paulo

- 5G rollout driving edge infrastructure needs
- 22M+ homes ready for FTTH provisioning on V.tal network
