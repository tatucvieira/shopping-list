## Overview
Latin America's largest neutral digital infrastructure company. Owner of the biggest fiber optic network in Brazil.

## Key Facts
- **Founded:** 2022 (from Oi SA's fiber infrastructure unit, UPI InfraCo)
- **HQ:** São Paulo, Brazil
- **Revenue:** ~$924M (Oct 2025 est.)
- **Employees:** ~2,100 across 3 continents
- **Network:** 450K+ km terrestrial fiber, 26K km submarine cables
- **FTTH Coverage:** 22M+ homes ready for provisioning
- **Ownership:** BTG Pactual PE (majority), GIC, CPP Investments

## Strategic Initiatives

### Synapse Submarine Cable
- US (New Jersey) to Brazil (São Paulo): 9,700 km
- 16 fiber pairs, 320 Tbps initial capacity, up to 800 Gbps/circuit
- SDM + Open Cable technology
- Construction: H2 2026 | RFS: 2029-2030
- Target: hyperscale cloud, AI workloads, content platforms

### Um Telecom Acquisition
- Announced: Dec 29, 2025
- Adds: 20K km fiber, 200+ cities, 1K digital solutions clients
- Regulatory approval: Expected Q1 2026
- Price: Undisclosed

### Renewable Energy Transition
- 16-year agreement with Atlas Renewable Energy + Atiaia Renováveis
- 10 solar plants, 947 GWh/year, starting 2026
- 100% renewable operations for V.tal + Tecto
- CO₂ reduction: 4M+ tonnes by 2041

### DFC Financing
- US$630M from US Development Finance Corporation
- Purpose: Fiber network expansion in Brazil

### Malbec Cable Extension
- Partnership with Meta
- 280 km branch to Porto Alegre
- Completion: 2027
- First subsea system in Porto Alegre
