## Holding Structure

### V.tal
- Largest neutral fiber optic network in Brazil: 450K+ km fiber, 26K km subsea cables
- ~$924M annual revenue (Oct 2025), ~2.1K employees across 3 continents
- 22M+ homes FTTH-ready
- Controlled by BTG Pactual PE funds, with GIC and CPP Investments as co-investors
- Key initiative: Synapse submarine cable (US-Brazil, 320 Tbps, RFS 2029-2030)
- Acquired Um Telecom (Dec 2025) — 20K km fiber, 200+ cities, regulatory approval expected Q1 2026
- Secured US$630M DFC financing for fiber expansion
- R$12B+ accounting adjustment announced Jan 2026
- 100% renewable energy starting 2026 (10 solar plants, 947 GWh/year)
- IPO candidate per BTG Pactual partner Pedro Henrique Fragoso

### Nio
- Fiber broadband ISP born from Oi Fibra customer base acquisition
- ~4M subscribers across Brazil
- Operates on V.tal's neutral FTTH network but has independent management/governance
- HQ: São Paulo, office in Rio de Janeiro
- Joint winner (with TIM) for Broadband Consistent Quality at 74.1% (Opensignal, Oct 2025)
- Focus: differentiated B2C and SMB fiber broadband

### Tecto
- Data center subsidiary of V.tal, $1B investment plan
- Led by Pedro Henrique Fragoso (BTG Pactual partner) with dedicated structure and governance
- Facilities in Brazil and Colombia
- Fortaleza (Praia do Futuro): 13K sqm, 20MW, Phase 1 live Q4 2025
- Porto Alegre (TPOA1): R$200M investment, 3MW initial scaling to 20MW
- Expansion pipeline: Santana de Parnaíba (SP)
- Partnership with Meta on Malbec cable extension to Porto Alegre (2027)

### BTG Pactual
- Controlling shareholder since May 2024
- Hands-on via Pedro Henrique Fragoso (Tecto lead, IPO commentator)
- Co-investors: GIC (Singapore), CPP Investments (Canada)
